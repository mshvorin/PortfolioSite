About

What I’m good at

I tend to do well when there are a lot of moving parts and no perfectly clean path forward. Old systems, messy workflows, heavy SQL, backend logic, data work, frontend cleanup, I’m comfortable stepping into whatever the project needs.

The work I’m proudest of usually helps in a real way. It saves time, removes friction, cuts down on mistakes, or makes something less frustrating for the people using it.

I also like working closely with people to understand what they actually need, turn that into a real plan, and explain clearly how we’re going to do it. Once we know the goal, I’m someone who sticks with it until it’s done right.

What matters to me

Being useful to a team matters to me. That can mean building something new, improving something old, or stepping into a problem that just needs ownership and follow-through.

Adapting quickly has become one of my strengths. Learning new tools, picking up the shape of a codebase, and getting productive fast is a big part of how I work.

Two Google mentorships through Basta and a mentorship with Yext were a big part of my growth. They helped me think more seriously about system design, engineering standards, and the kind of engineer I want to keep becoming.
